A next-of-kin box is one findable place that gathers the information the people
you trust would need to act on your behalf — where your important documents and
accounts are, who to contact, and how to reach what matters. It is not a legal
document or a product to buy. It is simply an organized answer to the question
your loved ones would otherwise have to piece together on their own.

This guide is for anyone who would rather leave that answer ready than leave it
to chance. You do not need to be old, ill, wealthy, or a legal expert. If people
depend on you — or would be the ones sorting things out — a box helps.

## It's for two moments, not just one

People often think of a box as something for after a death. It matters just as
much for the times you are still here but cannot manage things yourself — a
hospital stay, an accident, an illness that takes your attention for a while.

- **If you can't speak for yourself for a time**, the right people can keep your
  life running: pay what needs paying, reach who needs reaching, and make
  decisions with some sense of what you would want.
- **If you die**, the same box spares the people grieving you from a stressful
  scavenger hunt on top of their loss.

You are building for both. Most of what you gather serves either situation, so
you do not have to plan for each one separately.

## Two ways to begin

There is no single right pace. Pick the path that fits the time and energy you
have right now — you can always switch.

- **I have an afternoon.** Start with the
  [Quick start](/guide/getting-started). It walks you through the smallest
  useful box — the handful of things that carry the most weight — so you leave
  something genuinely helpful behind today, even if it is not complete.
- **I want to be thorough.** Work through the [full guide](/guide) section by
  section. Each part covers what to gather and why, in plain language, and you
  can take the sections in any order.

Either way, a partial box is far better than none, and you can add to it a little
at a time.

## What to expect

- **Work at your own pace.** Nothing here is urgent or all-or-nothing. Save what
  you have, come back when you can, and keep your box somewhere the right people
  can find it.
- **This is education, not legal advice.** OpenNOK explains how to organize your
  own information. It does not draft wills or directives, and the rules for those
  differ from one place to another. When you need documents like those, a
  qualified professional can help you put them in place.
- **Nothing you enter leaves your device.** This site has no accounts and no
  backend. Anything you type or check off stays in your own browser — we never
  see it. Your box is yours, kept on your own terms.

## What's in the guide

The full guide breaks the work into small sections and points you to plain-language
help along the way. The [glossary](/glossary) keeps definitions of the less-familiar
terms — words like *executor* and *directive* — in one place, so nothing here
assumes you already speak the language.

As the guide grows, it will also include printable checklists you can fill in by
hand, a short page written for the person who eventually opens your box, and more
about the project behind this site. For now, the two paths above are the place to
start.

There is no rush. The best box is the one that exists — begin with whatever you
can do today.
